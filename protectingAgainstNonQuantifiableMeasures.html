You Might (and Probably Will) Get Called Racist

One of the most effective tools wielded by Woke participants is the credible threat that no matter what you say, you might get called racist. It's effective for a number of reasons. First being called (and more importantly being seen as) racist is one of the worst accusations that people can receive, and it can easily lead to serious consequences. Second, it's very easy to do. Third defending against it is difficult partly because people can easily doubt themselves "maybe I actually am racist?" Finally, it's difficult to defend against because a typical response to denying that you're racist is, well, to maintain that denial implies that you are a racist. (This last tactic is called a Kafka trap.)

So what should one do about this? Well, it's worth understanding the definition of racism, how it compares to the woke definition of racism, and what this implies about being accused of being racist by a Woke participant.

Commonplace Definition of Racism

The definition of racism is straightforward and well understood. The Oxford English Dictionary definition: "Prejudice, discrimination, or antagonism by an individual, community, or institution against a person or people on the basis of their membership of a particular racial or ethnic group, typically one that is a minority or marginalized." According to merriam-webster.com it's "A belief that race is a fundamental determinant of human traits and capacities and that racial differences produce an inherent superiority of a particular race." Together, these definitions incorporate three different notions commonly understood to be racism. First, individuals conduct racism and behave in a racist fashion. Second, racism involves discriminating against someone according to their "racial" or ethnic group. Third, racism implies a worldview that some races or ethnicities are inherently superior to others. If you believe such things, than you can fairly be described as a racist. If you don't, and in particular if you adhere to traditional liberal ideas of universality that all people should be treated the same regardless of their racial or ethnic group (among other groups or identities to which they may belong), you cannot fairly be described as a racist.

Woke Definition of Racism

The Woke definition of racism on the other hand is somewhat more involved. It is summarized succinctly in the merriam-webster.com definition as follows: "the systemic oppression of a racial group to the social, economic, and political advantage of another, specifically: White Supremacy." This is consistent with a typical academic Critical Social Justice definition (Ozlem and Diangelo 2012) where racism is held to be "White racial and cultural prejudice and discrimination, supported by institutional power and authority, used to the advantage of Whites and the disadvantage of people of Color. Racism encompasses economic, political, social, and institutional actions and beliefs that systematize and perpetuate an unequal distribution of privileges, resources, and power between Whites and people of Color." There are a number of characteristics of this definition that make it different and in some cases completely contradictory to the commonly understood definition.

First, racism is uniquely white. That is, only white people can be racist to people who are not white. The reason for this is that packed into the definition is the notion that racism needs to be supported by institutional power, which according to the perspective is something only white people have. Moreover, while racism is something that individuals can participate in, the term is used to encompass all social and cultural constructs including institutions that (it is said) conspire, and together, explain any unequal distribution of resources between white and non-white people. Second, since people are subjects of their cultures and behave in such a way as to perpetuate existing structures of power, they don't have any agency in whether or not they conduct racism; they do whether they mean to or not.
