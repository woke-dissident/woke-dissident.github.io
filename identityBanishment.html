Identity Banishment

This is my first post after the release of the manual "How to Protect against Wokecraft in Universities." Until now, I had only included posts that would fit neatly into the manual. That meant among other things that when identifying woke strategies or tactics, I would only include those that I had actually observed or for which a public record existed. Today I'm writing about a tactic I've come across that I've not observed explicitly in universities and for which I can't find a public record. I'm discussing, however, because I think it is an interesting tactic that contributes to the spread of woke. I'm calling the tactic "identity banishment."

I, like many, have witnessed the tactic, but it didn't really stand out to me until reading Douglas Murray's The Madness of Crowds. He identifies but doesn't name the tactic. Two examples help to illustrate the tactic. The first relates to Peter Thiel, the venture capitalist and co-founder of PayPal.

As reported by Murray, on July 21st, Peter Thiel spoke at the main hall of the Republican National Convention in Cleveland and said "I am proud to be gay. I am proud to be a Republican. But most of all, I am proud to be an American." This was met with various reactions from various milieux but the one Murray mentions is that of an essay by Prof. Jim Downs in the Advocate (the oldest and largest LGBT publication in the US according to Wikipedia - I couldn't find an "About" page for the Advocate). In the article, Downs states:

Thiel is an example of a man who has sex with other men, but not a gay man. Because he does not embrace the struggle of people to embrace their distinctive identity.

A second example, also related to the Republicans, is Kanye West, the billionaire US rapper, music producer and former spouse of Kim Kardashian. In October of 2018, Kanye West publicly came out in support of Donald Trump and even said that he loved Donald Trump.
